Arabika

Coffea arabica (/əˈræbɪkə/), also known as the Arabic coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the coffee and madder family Rubiaceae. It is believed to be the first species of coffee to have been cultivated, and is currently the dominant cultivar, representing about 60% of global production.

Robusta

Coffea canephora (syn. Coffea robusta, also known as robusta coffee) is a coffee species native to central and western Sub-Saharan Africa. It is a flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family. Though commonly referred to as Coffea robusta, the plant is formally known as Coffea canephora, and it comes in two varieties: robusta and nganda.

Liberika

Coffea liberica, often known as Liberian coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family that produces coffee. It is indigenous to western and central Africa, from Liberia to Uganda and Angola, and has become naturalized in the Philippines, Indonesia, Seychelles, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and Malaysia.
